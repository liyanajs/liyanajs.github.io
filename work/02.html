GIS SMALLWORLD

Utility Asset Management / Magik Development

GE Smallworld is the industry standard for managing complex utility networks, powered by its own proprietary object-oriented language: Magik. It is the digital backbone for major utility providers.


Mastering Magik is notoriously challenging. The standard training curriculum for new engineers is a rigorous six-month program. However, I accelerated this timeline, completing the full fast-track certification in just four months. This allowed me to deploy early into live projects, contributing immediately to the backend architecture for national-scale infrastructure.

CLIENT Tenaga Nasional Berhad (TNB)
YEAR 2022-2024
TECH STACK
Magik (OOP) GE Smallworld Oracle Spatial

Backend Plugin Engineering

Working directly under Tenaga Nasional Berhad (TNB), I was tasked with extending the core capabilities of their electrical management system. This involved writing custom backend plugins in Magik to handle specific data workflows requested by the utility engineers.


Because these plugins manage critical electrical assets, precision was non-negotiable. I handled the full development lifecycle, from interpreting client requirements to coding the logic and documenting technical release notes for deployment.

System Maintenance & Stability

Beyond new development, I transitioned into the core maintenance team responsible for the stability of the entire plugin ecosystem. This role required deep debugging skills to trace issues within the legacy codebase.


I investigated and resolved high-priority bugs reported by TNB users, optimizing existing code to ensure the national grid's data management remained uninterrupted and accurate.
